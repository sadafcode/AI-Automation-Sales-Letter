Here is the complete guide for Step 1 — Identify Your #1 Automation Opportunity and Design the AI Solution

Step 1 is where everything begins. Most businesses jump straight to building without knowing what to build. This guide shows you exactly how to find your best automation opportunity and design the solution before touching a single tool.

Phase 1: Business Process Audit — List Every Manual Task (1–2 Hours)
  1. Open a Google Sheet. Create 5 columns: Task Name | Who Does It | Time Per Week (hours) | What Goes Wrong Manually | Customer Impact
  2. Walk through a typical week in your business and list every task your team does repeatedly — answering the same questions, sending reminders, checking orders, updating spreadsheets, booking appointments, sending reports
  3. For each task: fill in how many hours per week it takes and what goes wrong when it is done manually (e.g. "slow response means customers go elsewhere" or "human error in data entry causes wrong orders")
  4. Also list the customer-facing impact: Does a slow task make customers wait? Does a missed task mean a lost sale? Does an error hurt the customer experience?
Phase 2: Calculate the True Cost of Each Task
  1. For each task: multiply hours per week × 4 (weeks) × hourly cost of the person doing it. Example: If a customer support task takes 20 hours/week and the team member earns $5/hour, the monthly cost is $400.
  2. Add the revenue cost: estimate how many customers are lost each month because of this task being done slowly or manually. Multiply by your average customer value. Example: 10 missed leads × $500 average order = $5,000 lost monthly.
  3. Your true monthly cost = staff cost + lost revenue. This is what AI automation saves you.
  4. Write this number in the last column of your Google Sheet. This becomes your ROI proof when the automation is live.
Phase 3: Rank and Select Your First Automation
  1. Score each task on 3 criteria (1–5 each): (1) How much time does it waste? (2) How much revenue does it cost? (3) How repetitive/predictable is it? (Predictable tasks are easiest to automate.)
  2. Add the 3 scores. The task with the highest total score is your first automation target.
  3. Rule of thumb — the best first automations are: customer WhatsApp/email responses, appointment booking, order tracking, lead follow-ups, or data entry between two systems
  4. Do not try to automate everything at once. One automation done well delivers more results than five done poorly.
Phase 4: Write Your AI Solution Brief (30 Minutes)
  1. Write a one-page document answering: What is the exact problem? (be very specific) Who triggers it? (e.g. "customer sends a WhatsApp message") What should the AI do? (e.g. "read the message, understand intent, respond with correct information or book appointment") What should the output be? (e.g. "WhatsApp reply sent within 15 seconds")
  2. List the systems involved: Where does the input come from? (WhatsApp, email, form) Where does the output go? (WhatsApp reply, Google Calendar, spreadsheet)
  3. List what the AI needs to know: Your services, prices, opening hours, booking process, FAQ answers
  4. This brief is what you hand to a developer — or follow yourself using Make.com and Claude API (see Step 3 guide for exact build instructions)
Output of Step 1: A clear brief document that specifies exactly what to build — so there is no guessing when you start building in Step 3
